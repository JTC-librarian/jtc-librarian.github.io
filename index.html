Welcome

I am the Discovery and Systems Manager at Solent University Library in Southampton, UK. As well as being a librarian, I am a keen hiker, a mediocre pub cricketer, and an amateur classicist. I live in Southampton with my wife Catherine, a professional pianist whom I met through a London-based walking club. Our son Nicholas was born in 2021.

Librarianship

At Solent I am a member of the Library Management Team and I have ultimate responsibility for the Cataloguing & Metadata and the Digitization & Reading List teams. I serve as the library's Systems Librarian, which is probably the most time-consuming part of my role, and lead the library on copyright policy. I have previously worked in metadata and/or systems at Durham University, King's College London, and University College London. I am interested in making things easier for librarians, by automating routine tasks, and for library users, by improving metadata sharing, structure, and display.

Classics

My undergraduate degree was in Classics, that is Greek and Latin Language and Literature, and I continue to read around the subject. My greatest interest is in Greek tragedy, in trying to understand how it worked both formally and in its effect on the original audience. I have found Malcolm Heath's The Poetics of Greek Tragedy and Oliver Taplin's The Stagecraft of Aeschylus particularly valuable. I have published several articles on tragedy myself, each one dealing either with stage action or the sound of tragedy. They all work in the same way: I identify a pattern that is unlikely to have occurred by chance and draw the natural conclusions. See my publications page for a list of these articles.
